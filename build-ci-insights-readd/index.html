The Problem

Entrepreneurship and innovation are hard, until you make them a repeatable process. Below are some of the most common problems we’ve seen across startups and organizations, based on our work with them:

Innovation isn’t embedded as a cultural value

Change meets resistance; efforts feel sporadic and engagement stays low.

Global engagement ~20–23%, indicating persistent culture headwinds.

Gallup — State of the Global Workplace 2024

Culture + Innovation Mindset

Strategy is treated as a static document

Short-term pivots multiply; OKRs drift and teams lose direction.

Roughly 70% of employees report misalignment with strategy.

Forbes — When strategy fails to land

Planning Mindset + Leadership Development

Decisions lack reliable evidence and testing

ROI suffers as opinions outrun data and validated learning.

Data-driven orgs are far more likely to improve decisions.

Harvard Business School Online — Data-driven decisions

Evidence-Based Decision-Making

Innovation leadership lacks ownership and cadence

Projects stall, alignment slips, and dependency risks increase.

Transformations fail without visible leadership and governance.

McKinsey — Why transformations fail

Leadership & Innovation Governance

Rigid hierarchies and outdated skill structures

Cycles slow down and teams detach from real user needs.

Greater autonomy correlates with higher productivity and ownership.

Core processes can’t scale or adapt reliably

Costs creep, burnout rises, and delivery becomes inconsistent.

Scaling well requires deliberate operating-model redesign.
