# i18n readiness — doulab.net — 2026-07 (post ES-launch)

## Scope

Read-only audit of EN/ES parity, glossary discipline, locale UX, SEO surfacing, and overflow risk on `doulab.net` immediately after the bilingual launch (commit `eb1c8c8`, 2026-06-01). Bilingual rollout puts EN at `/*` and ES at `/es/*`. Coverage:

- **Parity**: every `src/pages/**` route, every `docs/**` MDX, every `blog/**` post mirrored under `i18n/es/**`.
- **Chrome translations**: `navbar.json`, `footer.json`, `code.json`, `docusaurus-plugin-content-blog/options.json`, `docusaurus-plugin-content-docs/current.json`.
- **Glossary discipline** across the parallel-agent translation batches (E-I2-R1 umbrella).
- **Routing & SEO**: locale dropdown deep-link behavior; `build/sitemap.xml` vs `build/es/sitemap.xml`; absence of `hreflang` alternates; `htmlLang` per locale.
- **Localized chrome surfaces**: 404 page, error pages, admonition labels, blog UI strings, theme-classic strings.
- **Form labels, error messages, CTA labels** in ES.
- **Date/time/currency formatting**: CHF 150 display, last-updated dates.
- **Long-string overflow** risk in hero subtitles, CTA buttons, card headings (viewport sweep `viewport-2026-07-prod-v1/`).
- **Untranslated fragments** leaking English into ES (intentional product names vs accidental EN bleed).

Out of scope: translation labor itself; the E-V4 doubled blog-tag URL bug (filed under E-V4, surfaces in both sitemaps); content/copy strategy beyond glossary; ES-specific SEO (handled in `05-seo.md`).

## Method

1. Read `docusaurus.config.ts` (i18n block, navbar/footer, locale labels).
2. Enumerated `src/pages/**` (26 EN files) vs `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-pages/**` (26 ES files). Confirmed 1:1 mirror.
3. Enumerated `docs/research-resources/**` + `docs/intro.mdx` + `docs/releases.mdx` (16 user-facing files) vs `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-docs/current/**`. Confirmed mirror.
4. Enumerated `blog/**` (4 posts) vs `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-blog/**`. Confirmed mirror.
5. Read all five chrome JSON files. Counted untranslated keys (English fallthrough) and stub artefacts.
6. Greped ES corpus for glossary candidates: `Gate` family ("compuerta" vs "punto de control" — 216 vs ~10), `case studies` family ("casos de estudio" vs "casos de éxito" — 49 vs ~25), CTA verbs ("Agenda" vs "Reserva" vs "Programa" — mixed), IMM-P® stage names, role/methodology terms.
7. Greped ES corpus for English fragments: Mermaid node labels, "Stripe Checkout", "lawful basis", "What we do / Who it's for / How it works" headings, "Book a … online" labels embedded in copy.
8. Inspected `build/sitemap.xml` and `build/es/sitemap.xml` for cross-locale hreflang, ES URL inclusion, sitemap-index pattern.
9. Inspected viewport sweep `ops/audits/doulab-net/viewport-2026-07-prod-v1/{en,es}/` for the six widths (360, 390, 768, 1280, 1366, 1920) on 18 pages.
10. Verified the locale dropdown navbar config and `htmlLang` per locale.

## Findings (ranked)

### I18N-001 — Glossary drift: "Gate" rendered three ways across ES corpus (umbrella for E-I2-R1)

- Severity: **P0**. Impact: **5**. Effort: **M**.
- Location: 32 files; primary offenders `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-pages/case-studies/{afp-siembra,alpha-inversiones,fundapec,ogtic-redlab}.tsx`, `services/{innovation-maturity,clarityscan,clarityscan/audit,clarityscan/diagnostic,imm-dt}.tsx`, `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-blog/2025-09-12-clarityscan-decision-latency.md`.
- Observation: The IMM-P® term **"Gate"** (decision checkpoint between Discovery → Validation → Efficiency → Scale → Continuous Improvement) is rendered in three competing ways inside the ES corpus:
  - **"compuerta"** — 216 occurrences across 32 files (dominant in case studies + blog + service pages + Innovation Lab Guide).
  - **"punto de control"** — used as the canon on the case-studies index page (`i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-pages/case-studies/index.tsx:18,76,98`).
  - **"gate"** (English, lowercased in ES prose) — `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-pages/contact/index.tsx:185` ("Plan por etapas con gates claros…").
- Why this is P0: this is the single most repeated piece of IMM-P® terminology on the site. A buyer who reads a service page, a case study and a blog post in one session will see all three variants. It erodes the framework's authority signal, which is the entire commercial proposition.
- **Recommendation — canonical term: "compuerta"** (with `gate` in parentheses on first mention per page).
  - **Rationale (LATAM business Spanish):** "compuerta" is the term IMM-P® clients (AFP Siembra, Alpha Inversiones, FUNDAPEC, OGTIC/RedLab — all Dominican Republic) already use in workshops and deliverables, mirrored in the four case-study ES pages translated by the agents who had access to the engagement materials. "Punto de control" reads as a generic project-management term (PMI-style) and dilutes the IMM-P® brand vocabulary; it also overlaps with "control point" in audit/compliance contexts, which is a different concept. "Gate" (English) breaks the locale promise.
  - Replace the 10 instances of "punto de control" on `case-studies/index.tsx` with "compuerta". Replace "gates" on `contact/index.tsx:185` with "compuertas".
  - Update the glossary: add **Gate → compuerta (gate)** with the parenthetical bilingual gloss on first mention per page; keep "compuerta" thereafter.
- Evidence: 216 vs ~10 vs 1 occurrence counts; `case-studies/index.tsx:18,76,98`; `case-studies/afp-siembra.tsx:135,175,176,178,179,181,182,211,226,301`; `services/innovation-maturity.tsx` (22 occurrences); `contact/index.tsx:185`.

### I18N-003 — Glossary drift: ClarityScan® CTA verb is inconsistent ("Agenda" vs "Reserva")

- Severity: **P1**. Impact: **4**. Effort: **S**.
- Location: 24 ES pages contain a `Reserva`/`Agenda`/`Programa` variant.
- Observation: The same CTA action (start a ClarityScan Level 1 via Stripe Checkout) ships with three different verbs in ES:
  - **"Agenda un ClarityScan® en línea"** — `about/index.tsx:136,279`, `case-studies/afp-siembra.tsx:123`, `case-studies/alpha-inversiones.tsx:108`, `case-studies/fundapec.tsx:104`, `case-studies/ogtic-redlab.tsx:105`, `case-studies/index.tsx:86`, `work-with-us/index.tsx:98`, `index.tsx:654,674`, `contact/index.tsx:81,86,202`.
  - **"Reserva Nivel 1: CHF 150"** / **"Reserva un Snapshot Nivel 1…"** — `services/clarityscan.tsx:85,367`, `services/clarityscan/diagnostic.tsx:262`, `services/index.tsx` (book-online card aria).
  - **"Programa un ClarityScan®"** — `blog/2025-09-12-clarityscan-decision-latency.md:94` ("[Programa un ClarityScan®]").
  - Plus **"Empieza con ClarityScan®"** as the cold-start CTA on the home hero (`index.tsx:650,673`) and footer (`footer.json:35`).
- Why this matters: ClarityScan is the site's primary lead-conversion CTA. The verb is the action label. Three different verbs (Agenda, Reserva, Programa) trigger three different mental models: *Agenda* = booking a meeting, *Reserva* = paying for a slot, *Programa* = scheduling. The literal action is **pay-then-book** (Stripe Checkout → Microsoft Bookings), so the verb mismatch creates friction at the highest-intent click on the site.
- **Recommendation — canonical: "Reserva ClarityScan® Nivel 1 (CHF 150)"** for the paid Stripe checkout CTA. **"Agenda una llamada de descubrimiento"** stays for the free discovery call (different funnel).
  - **Rationale (LATAM business Spanish):** "Reservar" matches the commercial transaction (you reserve a paid slot, like a hotel or a flight); "Agendar" matches a free booking; "Programar" is the most ambiguous and least transactional. Stripe's own ES locale uses "Reservar" for paid bookings. Keeping the cold-start hero CTA as "Empieza con ClarityScan®" is fine — it is a navigational verb, not a transactional one.
  - Sweep all 24 pages and unify: pay-CTA → "Reserva", call-CTA → "Agenda", informational → "Conoce" / "Explora" / "Lee".
- Evidence: occurrence counts above; specific lines cited.

### I18N-005 — `build/sitemap.xml` has zero ES URLs and zero `hreflang` alternates

- Severity: **P0**. Impact: **5**. Effort: **S**.
- Location: `build/sitemap.xml` (EN-only, 65 URLs); `build/es/sitemap.xml` (ES-only, 65 URLs); no `sitemap_index.xml` published.
- Observation:
  - The Docusaurus sitemap plugin runs **per-locale** and emits two separate sitemaps at `build/sitemap.xml` and `build/es/sitemap.xml`. Neither contains the other locale's URLs.
  - **Neither sitemap declares `<xhtml:link rel="alternate" hreflang="…">` alternates.** The `xmlns:xhtml` namespace is declared in the root element but never used in any `<url>` entry.
  - There is **no `sitemap_index.xml`** that lists both per-locale sitemaps for Google to discover.
  - There is **no `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="…">` in the HTML `<head>`** of either locale's pages (Docusaurus does not auto-emit this).
  - `robots.txt` points only at `https://www.doulab.net/sitemap.xml`; no path to the ES sitemap.
- Why this is P0: Google will discover the ES sitemap only via the locale-prefix path it crawls, and without `hreflang` alternates it will treat EN and ES versions as either duplicate content or unrelated pages. ES will not rank for Spanish queries even though the content exists. This is the single largest SEO regression introduced by the launch.
- **Recommendation**:
  1. Publish a `sitemap_index.xml` at the site root that references both `https://www.doulab.net/sitemap.xml` and `https://www.doulab.net/es/sitemap.xml`. Update `robots.txt` to point there.
  2. Inject `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en" href="…">` + `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="es" href="…">` + `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="…">` into every page `<head>`. Use a Docusaurus swizzled `Root` or a custom plugin (Docusaurus does not ship this out of the box; the `@docusaurus/plugin-sitemap` config option for hreflang has historically been minimal).
  3. Inject `<xhtml:link>` `hreflang` alternates into each sitemap `<url>` entry (Docusaurus `plugin-sitemap` supports this via the `lastmod`/`changefreq` options plus a manual post-processing step, or via the third-party `@docusaurus/plugin-sitemap` fork if needed).
- Hand-off to **05-seo.md**: this finding overlaps with the SEO role's scope. Filed here because it is the i18n role's primary surfacing duty; coordinate severity ranking.
- Evidence: `build/sitemap.xml` (read in full — no `xhtml:link` elements); `build/es/sitemap.xml` (read in full — same); no `sitemap_index.xml` in `build/`.

### I18N-012 — Date/time formatting + currency: CHF 150 OK; last-updated date format may not localize

- Severity: **P2**. Impact: **2**. Effort: **S**.
- Location: `i18n/es/docusaurus-plugin-content-pages/services/clarityscan.tsx:43-44` (Schema.org Offer); various ES pages.
- Observation:
  - **CHF 150**: rendered consistently as **"CHF 150"** in ES (15+ occurrences across home, services, about, insights). This is correct — CHF is the ISO-4217 code and works in both locales. Schema.org Offer uses `price: '150'`, `priceCurrency: 'CHF'`. **No action needed.**
  - **Last-updated dates** on doc pages (`showLastUpdateAuthor: true`, `showLastUpdateTime: true` in `docusaurus.config.ts:74-75`): Docusaurus formats these using the page's locale automatically via Intl.DateTimeFormat. ### I18N-013 — No CI guard against future EN pages without ES mirrors

- Severity: **P1**. Impact: **3**. Effort: **S**.
- Location: build process; `package.json` scripts.
- Observation: Docusaurus's fallback behavior (per `docusaurus.config.ts:28-29` comment) is to **silently serve the EN source for any page that does not exist in `i18n/es/`**. This means a future PR that adds `src/pages/services/new-thing.tsx` without an ES mirror will (a) build green, (b) ship to production, (c) render the new page in **English on the ES locale** with no warning. Fail the build with the list of missing files. Mirror the pattern from `verify:build`.
- Evidence: `docusaurus.config.ts:28-29` (comment explicitly confirms silent fallback).
